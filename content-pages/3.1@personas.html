Generated personas

How we boosted the time to value in Contentsquare platform by using a sessions clustering model.

Context.

If you don’t know, Contentsquare is a User Experience analytics platform. It takes a quantitative approach to help users understand their audience navigation, intentions and perception on their websites and apps.

CS aims at
detecting intents

And the platform
looks like this

This tool within Contentsquare shows all journeys taken on the website for the set of sessions selected.

The very base (and power) of Contentsquare tool is segmentation, used in what we call the “Analysis Context”.

It allows users to filter sessions based on device, date range, and a set of more or less complex conditions to refine users behaviors.

The analysis context

It appears at the top of any tool on the platform.
It’s THE entry point to start an analysis.

It allows advanced filtering

Here, filtering sessions that connected for the 2nd time, where users viewed a product page and are coming from France, Spain or Portugal.

I know, it’s tough.

Opportunities.

We needed to come up with a more advanced, more intuitive and more pro-active way to let our users analyse their Personas.

_11% of our users just change the date in the Analysis context

_67% of our users pick a simple segment

_Lots of reports of false “bugs” where users simply don’t understand the set of conditions they built

_More than half of our users are already working with Personas

_Our users expect more “pro-activeness”, they want us to push some relevant insights

All that with one key outcome in mind:

Boost the “time to value”

centi

Meaning

How to make our users find relevant insights faster in the platform?

Implementation.

We knew that in order to bring at the same time:

_Easy access

_Intuitive

_Pro active

segments, we needed to opt for a different approach than the current “conditions based” one.

Fast forward several months of exploration with a data-science team, we opted for unsupervised clustering method.

Complex and expert terms to say we made groups of sessions based on their journey on the website.
All that using a clever algorithm to embed journey data into comparable “objects” for which we could calculate a distance between each other.

Simplified view of the algo

Generated personas was a very interesting topic, because I had to get into many data science details in order to connect the dots between what we wanted in the Product for end users and what the machine learning algo could provide.

The v1 solution

We came out with a first solution, a “Minimum Viable Product”, on which we could perform some tests with beta users.

Basically, this new tool surfaces automatically for users different groups of sessions based on their journey on the website (the “bubbles” on the graph).

Impact.

This first solution brung a lot of qualitative, positive feedbacks.

Backed by data, we noted:

_Adoption rate of 43%
(ratio between all beta users for this feature/those who accessed 3+ times)

_Stickiness rate of 28% on the first 4 weeks of the beta
(among beta users, those who accessed the feature 3+ times a week)

One major pain point: our user interviews pointed out the “time to value” did not increase significantly.

We then worked out a more refined solution, trying to further connect the dots between the gerenated personas and the rest of Contentsquare tool set.

The v2 solution

This solution is still being implemented, but first user tests led to very positive feedbacks.
