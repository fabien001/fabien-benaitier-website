Ship

I ship features, because that’s the only way to have any impact on users.

...and to eventually make money as a company.

Conception is merely to mitigate the risk.

When implementing a new feature on the product, the probability of this feature to miss users needs is always 50/50.

So better fail fast.

I mean sure, you have to make a proper work of user research to understand your users and their needs. Then you have to properly design the solution to those needs, in an iterative manner

Your are going to iteratively refine your product vision by testing it against real users, and discuss business and technical priorities around the table with Product Owners, Product Managers, Developers and Experts.

But all of that will at most *minimize* the risk of failing to ship the right feature, properly designed, to your target users.

At the end of the day, the only way to know *for sure* that this new feature will meet user needs and fullfill the defined KPIs, is to ship it to those users and watch how they use it (or not).

Basically you could also rush to development with almost no conception whatsoever.

You just have to assume the risk is much bigger.

That’s what very early stage startup do.

And they're right.

It’s the whole team's responsability.

And in my opinion, whatever the result is, success or failure, everyone is responsible for what is shipped.

No, it's not the Lead Developer's fault because he did not want to implement that brilliant neumorphic picture carousel you designed. Nor it is the Product Manager’s fault for misjudging the business value of the feature.

It’s everyone's responsability, and it’s everyone’s mission to get back around the table and keep iterating.
