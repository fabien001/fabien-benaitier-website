Discover

I do qualitative research,

quantitative research

or benchmarking.

I always tackle any topic with a discovery phase.

Nothing really original or fancy here.

It’s mainly about understanding and helping define the business strategy, and gathering users needs.

This is typically a rather messy and organic phase.

Check directly to the synthesis of my discovery phase for my website

To build this website, I started by the target users, by priority:

_Recruiters

_Design managers in tech

_Peer designers

Basically, this website is not for my grandma to get what I do for a living.

Then came outcomes:

_Reduce the time for target users to understand my profile

_Increase my reach, measured by:

_the number of unprompted messages for job offers

_the number of positive replies for a first touchpoint after a job application

Basically, I’m the product here.

Eventually, I took advantage of my contacts in the tech industry to get the thoughts of tech recruiters and managers on the things they look for and look at for a Product Designer profile like mine.

It got me three opportunities:

_My material is too scattered, with material all over the place, making it longer to gather all info for my target users who have little time to scan a profile

At the time I had a Linkedin, with a pdf résumé, a link to my website, that had just links to my pdf portfolio, and link to my medium, which was leading to my linkedin...

_My profile appears too junior, putting the emphasis on the accumulation of skills and projects and lacking demonstration of my strong points

At the time 50% of my A4 résumé was for listing raw skills.

My portfolio was a 16-pager-list of projects with very brief showcase.

_No synthesis: you had to deduce my philosophy, my way of working, my character from the various stuff you could gather

Apart from a medium article that I wrote for a specific project, not a single word about me.
