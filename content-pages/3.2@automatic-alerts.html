In addition to being an analysis platform, one key aspect of Contentsquare is its monitoring power.

Users can set alerts on various key metrics, like the conversion rate, the bounce rate...etc...

The power of this tool is it’s ability to monitor those metrics automatically, using a clever forecasting algorithm.

Opportunities.

When I joined Contentsquare, the Alerting system was just a Minimum Viable product:

_you set up an Alert, with your metric, and a threshold above or under which to be warned

_once a day max, this metric was checked against the threshold

_you received an email in case an alert was triggered

_that’s it.

A daily notification email for triggered alerts

At that time, business-wise, Contentsquare was trying to increase the upsell on it’s “find & fix” paid module.

This part of the product was not performing very well, and we were having a lot of churn: our users were writing off “find & fix” from their contract.

We were tasked with one outcome:

Increase the number of weekly active users on Automatic alerts.

Mostly to prevent churn.

And to further demonstrate value and ease upsell for other customers.

From a set of user interviews and product analysis, we noted:

_40% of users create only 1 alert.
The stickiness was low.

_90% of users create “automatic” alerts.
They don’t want to bother with a precise setup

_Users complained about the fact they could not easily share alerts

_Users pointed out the uselessness of receiving alerts only once a day, sometimes several hours after something significant happened

Implem
entation.

We eventually spent several weeks exploring solutions, with many guerilla tests and back & forth with the data-engineering team, to come out with a complete refactoring of the alerting system:

_A real time alerting system
(Users would receive an alert within 5 minutes of the threshold being triggered)

_A triggered/solved mechanism, to also let them know when things went back to normal

_A live, clear, visual way to analyse what happened

_A new object rights management for users to properly share insightful alerts and their notifications

Impact.

The new, refactored Automatic Alerts module was released in 2022. The bump in weekly active users starting early 2022 was probably due to the prior announcement of the fact this feature would be released.

Notable increase in Weekly Active Users

Thanks to this new system, we saw a notable jump in the number of weekly active users. Not a surprise but still a reward.
