We,

because I value too much my peers opinions to work alone.

I get it, that might sound lame and cheesy.

Always get feedback.

From all stakeholders.

As a Product Designer, peers opinions are harsh. You consciously spend a whole day crafting the *perfect* user flow for that new feature. Just to have some other Designer notice the one corner case you missed in your flow that's going to ruin your users experience 40% of the time.

Or it's a Developer who will point out the sheer amount of technical hassle it will be to develop your awesome neumorphic picture carousel.

Same carousel that, according to the Product Manager, is far from bringing the value matching the business goals for this feature.

I don’t remember of any time I did not make at least 3 iterations for a given feature, with feedback sessions with engineers, product managers, or designers.

Communication over processes.

That's why I usually value communication over processes. And the sooner the better. I constantly challenge my work against my teamates opinions or users.

In my opinion, a good organisation starts with the ease people have in reaching the right person at the right moment.

That's why I developed an "around the table" philosophy. As a Product Designer, I iteratively bring the tangible matter in the middle of this table, and eventually materialize the one product vision that takes into account everyone's point of view.

Around this table, there are usually Product Owners, Product Managers, Developers, and Experts.

And I make sure we gather around this same table as often as needed to work well and fast.

My role is basically to materialize the Product Vision from the three main inputs.

That’s my vision of the so-called “table”.
