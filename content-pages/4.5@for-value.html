My job consists in creating value for those end users. Basically so that they pay for the service or product we craft for them.

So business is crucial, it’s even the starting point.

In my latest experience at Contentsquare I further developed this understanding of business strategy.

Inspired by this book (from which most of the methodology we use at Contentsquare is derived), I developed the habit of always working from outcomes.

This book

Outcomes
before solutions.

As a Designer, the tendency is high to jump to solutions. Figma is always just a click away after all (and we love this tool).

But I never open Figma without defining with stakeholders opportunities to tackle.

Not a 35-slide deck no, not even a digital board with 57 clusters of post-its.

A clear, concise, prioritised list of sentences, stating what users troubles, needs or desires we intend to tackle, and for what business value.

Solutions can then be derived from those opportunities.
