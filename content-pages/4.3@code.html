it's a Product.

I do mockups though, and prototypes, and diagrams, and anything that helps building that product.

A good user experience starts with the technical aspect.

Figma is wonderful sure.

The very website on which you are reading this was designed using Figma.

By the way

See how I made it

You can even make fully functionnal prototypes, with smart animations between transitions.

And variants ! With which you can perfectly organise your design system.

But as beautiful and functionnal your Figma prototype can be, it will never *be* the real product your users get in their hands. In my opinion, from a user standpoint, a well thought user experience even starts with the technical aspect.

Designing for real users, for the real world.

Say you are designing an app to help users navigate to any subway in the world. Sure you can have the best user flow possible in your mockups. You even planned on putting little animations that comes as mp4 videos in order to help out the casual traveller around the trickiest stations of Paris or London subways.

Too bad, at 30 meters beneath the ground, 4G or even 5G networks are not so good. And the great user experience you could demo with your Figma prototype, with useful animations and all, eventually turns into a mess of spinning loaders in reality.

We ship code, not mockups, not prototypes, not video animations. If anyone asks me to demo the product I am working on, I will open up the app, not my Figma project.
